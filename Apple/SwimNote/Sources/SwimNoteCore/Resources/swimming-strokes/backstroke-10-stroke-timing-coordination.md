## Overview
Stroke timing and coordination integrate all other backstroke techniques into a unified, continuous motion. Arms move in opposition (when one enters, the other pulls), the kick rhythm synchronizes with the stroke cycle, and body rotation peaks at entry. Mastering timing is what separates good swimmers from great ones.

**Difficulty:** Hard

## Key Points to Remember
- **Continuous arm motion** - No pauses, no dead spots
- **Opposing arms** - When one enters, other is pulling
- **Kick rhythm** - 6-beat kick standard for sprints (3 kicks per arm); 2-beat or 4-beat for distance events
- **Rotation timing** - Rotation peaks at entry
- **Breathing rhythm** - Regular pattern with arms

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Dead spots** - Pauses in arm motion
- **Arms together** - Both arms in similar positions
- **Kick timing off** - Kicks not synchronized
- **Irregular breathing** - Breaks rhythm
- **Rotation timing wrong** - Rotation not matching stroke

## Specific Drills

| Drill | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **6-3-6 Timing** | 6 kicks side, 3 strokes, 6 kicks other side |
| **Continuous Stroke** | Eliminate all pauses |
| **Kick Timing** | Focus on kick rhythm with arms |
| **Tempo Trainer** | Use metronome for consistent rhythm |
| **Stroke Count** | Count strokes per length for efficiency |

## Competitive Drills

#### Drill 1: Metronome Swimming (4x200m set, use waterproof metronome or audible pool-side metronome)
**Self-Check:** Perceived tempo consistency and stroke count stability

> [!note] Targets
> - **Beginner/Intermediate:** Stay within ±1 beep of the metronome for most of the set
> - **Advanced:** Consistently sync entry with metronome beep for ≥90% of strokes
> - **Elite:** ≥95% of strokes synced within one metronome interval
>
> Self-check cue: Sync your hand entry exactly with the metronome beep
>
> **Note:** The "≥97% within 0.05s" target in the original document is unrealistically precise — sub-millisecond timing requires electronic measurement. Also, the claim that backstroke is "more consistent than freestyle" is unsubstantiated. Both strokes require similar rhythm consistency.

**Video Check (side-angle footage):**
- Kick-to-stroke ratio: Visible synchronization between kick cycle and arm cycle. **Correction:** The original stated "perfect 6-beat synchronization for all distances." This is incorrect — elite backstrokers use different kick patterns depending on event: 6-beat for sprints (50m, 100m), 2-beat or 4-beat for distance (200m+). The 6-beat kick is standard for sprints but NOT for all distances.
- No glide: Continuous motion with no visible "dead spot" between entry and catch. **Note:** Measuring ≤0.05s pauses requires ≥200fps video. For practical self-coaching, assess qualitatively: does the stroke flow continuously?
- Competitive target: No major rhythm disruption during turns or breathing changes

**Competitive Impact:** Tempo variation costs time per 100m. **Note:** The "0.1s per stroke costs ~1.2s per 100m" claim is a coaching estimate. The exact time cost depends on stroke rate, race distance, and how tempo variation manifests. Maintaining consistent tempo is a hallmark of efficient swimmers.

#### Drill 2: Opposing Arm Drill (4x100m, focus on arm opposition)
**Self-Check:** Count strokes where arms are in opposing positions (one entering, one exiting)

> [!note] Targets
> - Competitive target: Majority of strokes show clear opposition (one arm entering as other is exiting). **Note:** The "100% of strokes" target is unrealistic — minor timing variations are normal even in elite swimmers.
> - Self-check cue: Feel one arm entering as the other exits (good opposition)

**Video Check (front-angle footage):**
- Opposing arm motion: When one hand enters water, the other is mid-pull or at exit (no catch-up pause — arms in opposite phases)
- Continuous motion: Arms move continuously without extended pauses
- Competitive target: Comparable timing on both left and right sides

**Competitive Impact:** Proper opposition increases pull efficiency and maintains body balance. *The "15% pull efficiency increase" from the original document is a coaching estimate; actual gains depend on baseline technique quality.*
