## Overview

Proper body position is the foundation of breaststroke technique. The goal is to keep the body flat and streamlined at the water surface during the glide phase, with the head looking down at the pool bottom. The wave-style undulation used in elite technique lifts the body slightly during the pull phase and "dives" the upper body forward during kick and glide, creating wave-like motion that reduces drag compared to traditional flat style. Body position accounts for approximately 20% of the breaststroke learning challenge.

**Difficulty:** Easiest

## Key Points to Remember

- **Keep body flat and streamlined** - at surface during glide phase
- **Head looks down at pool bottom** - during glide, not forward (looking forward causes hip drop)
- **Press chest down slightly during glide** - raises hips to surface level
- **Hips stay high near the surface** - avoid arched back position
- **Core engaged throughout** - prevents relaxed, sagging midsection
- **Wave-style undulation (elite technique)** - body lifts slightly during pull phase, upper body "dives" forward during kick and glide, creating wave-like motion that reduces drag compared to traditional flat style

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Letting hips sink low** - creates massive drag, the most critical error in breaststroke
- **Looking forward** - causes immediate hip drop through see-saw effect
- **Head staying too high** - prevents proper streamline
- **Arched back position** - breaks body alignment
- **Relaxed, sagging midsection** - core must stay engaged throughout

## Specific Drills

| Drill | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Streamline Glide** | Push off wall in streamlined position, hold 5-10 seconds. Repeat 10x. |
| **Head Position Drill** | Swim breaststroke looking only at pool bottom. Use lane line as visual target. |
| **Chest Press Drill** | During glide, actively press chest down to raise hips. |
| **Float & Balance** | Practice horizontal floating with face in water, focus on relaxed, balanced position. |

## Competitive Drills

#### Drill 1: Breaststroke Streamline Glide (15m push-off)

**Self-Check:** Glide distance from push-off before needing to initiate first stroke

> [!note] Tiered Targets (Glide Distance Before Speed Decay)
> - **Beginner:** 5-7m before speed drops below useful racing pace
> - **Intermediate:** 7-10m
> - **Advanced:** 10-12m
> - **Elite:** 12-15m (world-class swimmers with exceptional streamlines)
>
> Why measure distance, not time? A push-off starts at ~1.8-2.2 m/s but breaststroke's high drag profile causes rapid deceleration. Distance shows how well your streamline minimizes drag.
>
> **Note:** Breaststroke glide distances are shorter than freestyle because of higher drag coefficient. The FINA 15m rule limits underwater swimming to 15m after starts and turns. Push-off speeds are consistent with biomechanics literature (Lyttle et al., 1998).
>
> Self-check cue: Count silently as you glide, stop counting when you feel your speed drop below 1m/s

**Video Check (side-angle footage):**
- Hip height: ≤5cm below water surface during full glide
- Streamline alignment: Head fully between biceps, arms pressed straight together, core tight
- Competitive target: Body angle ≤10 degrees from horizontal during glide

**Competitive Impact:** Each additional meter of glide per push-off saves roughly 0.3s per 100m breaststroke race.

#### Drill 2: Prone Float Hold (still water, arms at sides)

**Self-Check:** Hold flat prone position with face submerged, no kicking

> [!note] Tiered Targets (Prone Float Hold Duration)
> - **Beginner:** 15-20 seconds continuous hold, hips remain near surface
> - **Intermediate:** 20-30 seconds
> - **Advanced:** 30-45 seconds
> - **Elite:** ≥45 seconds continuous hold
>
> Self-check cue: If your hips drop below surface, your core is not engaged enough
>
> **Note:** These hold times are coaching benchmarks. Poor body position during the hold indicates the same core engagement issues that cause hip sag during actual swimming.

**Video Check (side-angle footage):**
- No body arch: Spine stays in neutral position, no lumbar curvature
- No hip sag: Hips stay within 5cm of surface for entire hold
- Competitive target: No leg movement to maintain position

**Competitive Impact:** Proper body position cuts drag significantly during glide phase, the most important part of breaststroke. Research using CFD and tow-tank testing shows poor head/hip alignment can increase drag by 15-25%. *(Note: the 30% figure sometimes cited in coaching literature represents a rough upper-bound estimate.)*
