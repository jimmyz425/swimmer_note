## Overview
Arm stroke entry in backstroke sets up the entire pull phase. The pinky finger enters first at slightly outside shoulder width with the arm fully extended. A clean, pinky-first entry with minimal splash ensures proper catch position and avoids creating unnecessary drag.

**Difficulty:** Moderate

## Key Points to Remember
- **Pinky-first entry** - Little finger enters water first for proper catch setup
- **Entry behind shoulder** - Arm extends straight back; entry point slightly outside shoulder width
- **Full extension** - Reach past shoulder line
- **Clean entry** - Minimal splash, hand slicing into water smoothly
- **Straight arm entry** - Arm straight when entering; no crossing midline

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Whole hand enters together** - Should be pinky-first for proper catch
- **Entry too wide** - Outside shoulder width increases drag
- **Entry too narrow** - Across centerline causes zigzag path
- **Bent arm entry** - Arm should be straight when entering
- **Slapping entry** - Creates splash and resistance

| Drill | Description |

## Competitive Drills

#### Drill 1: Marker Entry Drill (pool markers taped on deck at shoulder width)
**Self-Check:** Count percentage of entries that land approximately at your shoulder marker, little finger first

> [!note] Tiered Targets
> - **Beginner:** ≥70% of entries approximately at shoulder line
> - **Intermediate:** ≥85% of entries at shoulder line
> - **Advanced/Elite:** ≥95% of entries at shoulder line (no crossing center, no wide entry)
>
> Self-check cue: Feel your little finger slice into the water before any other part of your hand
>
> **Note:** These percentages are coaching heuristics for entry placement consistency. Accurately counting entry percentages during swimming is challenging without video analysis.

**Video Check (top-down footage):**
- Entry quality: Little finger first, minimal splash, hand slicing into water smoothly. **Note:** The "15-30 degrees entry angle" is a coaching estimate; actual entry angle varies by swimmer. The key is clean, pinky-first entry with minimal splash.
- Extension: Arm extends forward after entry (visible reach before catch begins). **Note:** The "≥8cm past entry point" is a coaching estimate. Precise centimeter-level measurement requires calibrated camera setup.
- Competitive target: No cross-over (hand never enters inside shoulder line)

**Competitive Impact:** Wide or cross-over entry creates a zig-zag swimming path, increasing total distance traveled. *Estimates suggest 3-5% additional distance per race, though actual impact depends on severity and frequency. Research on crossover entry (Walsh et al., 2006) shows it increases frontal drag and can contribute to shoulder impingement over time. The 4-6% figure in the original document is in the right range but represents a coaching estimate for moderate crossover.*

#### Drill 2: Extension Pause Drill (4x25m, pause 1s at full extension)
**Self-Check:** Stroke count per 25m when pausing vs normal swimming

> [!note] Targets
> - Competitive target: Similar stroke count to normal swimming. A small increase (1-2 strokes) is normal for this drill since the pause disrupts rhythm.
> - Self-check cue: Feel the "reach" with your core rotation, not just your arm

**Video Check (side-angle footage):**
- No over-reach: Arm does not extend excessively past shoulder line (comfortable full extension, not strained). **Note:** The "12cm past shoulder line" over-reach threshold is a coaching estimate to prevent shoulder impingement. Exact comfortable extension varies by arm length and shoulder flexibility.
- Clean entry: No splash, hand slices into water
- Competitive target: Smooth transition between extension and catch (no visible hesitation or "dead spot" in normal swimming)

**Competitive Impact:** Additional reach per stroke translates to fewer strokes per length. *The relationship "2cm extra reach = ~0.25s saved per 100m" is a coaching estimate. The relationship between reach and time savings is not linear — hydrodynamic factors mean gains diminish at extreme reach lengths.*
