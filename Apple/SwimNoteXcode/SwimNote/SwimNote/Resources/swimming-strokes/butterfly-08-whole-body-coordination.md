## Overview
Whole-body coordination unites all butterfly techniques into a single, continuous wave motion. The body undulation initiates from the chest and core, connecting arms, body, and legs as one unit. Relaxation during recovery and power during pull and kick create the seamless flow that distinguishes efficient butterfly from disjointed effort.
**Difficulty: Hardest**

## Key Points to Remember
- Body undulation INITIATES from chest and core
- Arms, body, and legs work as ONE connected unit
- The stroke is a continuous wave motion, not separate parts
- All phases flow smoothly into each other
- Core strength connects upper and lower body
- Relaxation during recovery, power during pull and kick

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Disconnected arm and leg movements
- Swimming "parts" instead of whole stroke
- Tension throughout stroke (causes fatigue)
- Focusing on one element while ignoring others
- Loss of rhythm after breathing

## Specific Drills
| Drill | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Full Stroke with Tempo Trainer** | Use tempo trainer at consistent beep rate. Match stroke to beep. |
| **Video Analysis** | Record swimming, analyze timing and coordination. Identify weak points. |
| **Butterfly with Fins** | Use fins to maintain proper timing while reducing fatigue. |
| **Short Distance Intervals** | Swim 15-25m butterfly with rest. Focus on perfect form, not distance. |
| **Contrast Drill** | Swim 25m intentionally wrong, then 25m correctly. Feel the difference. |
| **Count Strokes** | Count strokes per length. Fewer strokes with same speed = better efficiency. |

## Competitive Drills
#### Drill 1: 3-Kick Per Cycle Drill (4x50m, 3 dolphin kicks per arm cycle)
**Self-Check:** Stroke count per 25m when using 3 kicks vs normal 2 kicks

> [!tip] Tiered 3-Kick vs 2-Kick Stroke Difference
> | Level | Stroke Difference (3-kick vs 2-kick per 25m) | Notes |
> |-------|-----------------------------------------------|-------|
> | **Beginner** | 3-5 more strokes | Extra kick disrupts timing; focus on smoothness |
> | **Intermediate** | 2-3 more strokes | Developing extra-kick efficiency |
> | **Advanced** | 1-2 more strokes | Good body control with varied kick patterns |
> | **Elite** | ≤1 stroke | Maintains efficiency regardless of kick pattern |

- Self-check cue: Focus on keeping your entire body working as a single coordinated unit

**Video Check (side-angle footage):**
- Undulation wave: Single continuous wave travels from head to toe through entire cycle
- No body separation: Upper and lower body move as one connected unit, no lag between hips and shoulders
- Synchronization between all components (arms, legs, core, breathing) improves with level

**Competitive Impact:** Proper whole-body coordination -- with arms, legs, core, and breathing working as one connected unit -- reduces energy waste and increases endurance. The "25% energy waste reduction" figure is a coaching estimate; the actual benefit depends on how disconnected the swimmer's movements were before improving coordination.

#### Drill 2: All-Out Sprint Drill (6x25m, maximum effort, full stroke)
**Self-Check:** Time per 25m, stroke count per lap

> [!tip] Tiered Sprint 25m Targets
> | Level | Time per 25m (LCM) | Strokes per 25m | Notes |
> |-------|-------------------|-----------------|-------|
> | **Beginner** | 25-35s | 12-18 strokes | Focus on form, not speed |
> | **Intermediate** | 18-25s | 10-14 strokes | Building sprint power |
> | **Advanced** | 14-18s | 7-10 strokes | Competitive sprint speed |
> | **Elite** | 11.5-14s | 6-8 strokes | World-record pace averages ~11.5-12.5s/25m in 100m fly |
>
> *Reference: Caeleb Dressel's 49.45 WR (2021 Tokyo): first 50m ~22.9s. Kristof Milak's 1:50.34 WR (2022 Budapest): averages ~13.8s per 25m in 200m fly.*

- Self-check cue: Focus on smooth, powerful strokes, not just fast thrashing

**Video Check (side-angle footage):**
- Technique maintenance: All components (body position, catch, pull, kick, timing) remain intact at maximum speed -- Elite: no visible breakdown; Advanced: minor breakdown in final 5m; Beginner/Intermediate: noticeable form loss
- Stroke length: Maintains similar distance per stroke at sprint vs easy pace (Advanced/Elite: ≤10% reduction; Beginner: up to 30% reduction)
- Form maintained for entire 25m sprint at appropriate level

**Competitive Impact:** Technique breakdown during sprinting -- where form deteriorates under maximum effort -- can significantly reduce speed. The "10-15% speed loss" is a coaching estimate; the actual cost depends on how much the swimmer's technique degrades at race pace versus easy speed.
