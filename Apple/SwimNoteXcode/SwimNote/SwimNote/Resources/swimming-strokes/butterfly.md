> [!note] On Difficulty Rankings
> These rankings are general guidelines. Actual difficulty varies by swimmer:
> - **Dolphin Kick** (Level 2): Often harder for adult learners than it appears — hip-driven undulation is counterintuitive.
> - **Arm Recovery** (Level 5): Arguably easier than Breathing (Level 6) for some swimmers since it's visible and self-correctable.
> - **Full Coordination** (Level 8): Closely tied to Timing/Rhythm (Level 7). Many coaches treat these as one combined technique. If your timing is good, coordination often follows naturally.

## Recommended Practice Approach

### Beginners
1. Master body position and horizontal float
2. Develop dolphin kick on back and side
3. Practice arm movements separately
4. Combine single arm with kick

### Intermediate
1. Refine two-kick timing
2. Perfect breathing timing
3. Build stroke endurance (25m intervals)
4. Focus on rhythm and continuous motion

### Advanced
1. Fine-tune all phases for efficiency
2. Reduce stroke count per length
3. Increase tempo while maintaining form
4. Race pace training
5. Underwater dolphin kick development (maximize distance within 15m FINA/World Aquatics limit)

## Key Mental Cues

- **"Chest down, hips up"** - Body position
- **"Two kicks"** - Never forget the second kick
- **"Breathe on the push"** - Timing the breath
- **"Wave motion"** - Whole body connected
- **"Low recovery"** - Save energy on recovery
- **"Enter-kick, Push-kick"** - Rhythm timing

### Image References

**Key Positions to Visualize:**

1. **Body Undulation Wave Motion**
   ```
   CHEST ↓ → HIPS ↑ → LEGS ↓

   Chest presses down → Hips rise → Kick whips down
   ```
   - Wave flows through entire body
   - Think: "Body is a dolphin" - continuous wave motion

2. **Two-Kick Cycle (Critical)**
   ```
   KICK 1 (smaller) → KICK 2 (larger)

   Kick 1: Timing with arm ENTRY
   Kick 2: Timing with arm PUSH
   ```
   - NEVER single kick per stroke
   - Think: "Enter-kick, Push-kick" - say it aloud

3. **Arm Entry Position**
   - Hands shoulder-width apart
   - Fingertips enter first
   - Arms reach forward slightly
   - Think: "Slide hands in, don't splash"

4. **High Elbow Catch**
   - Elbow above hand
   - Forearm vertical
   - Hands anchor water
   - Think: "Anchor forearm, pull body past it"

5. **Low Recovery Position**
   - Arms sweep low over water
   - Slightly bent, relaxed
   - Lead with thumbs
   - Think: "Skim the surface, don't lift high"

6. **Breathing Position**
   - Chin just above water
   - NOT lifting head high
   - Breathe during PUSH phase
   - Think: "Chin on water, not head in air"
