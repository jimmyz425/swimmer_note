# Freestyle (Front Crawl) Swimming Techniques
## Complete Reference Guide - Ranked from Easy to Hard to Master

> Part of [[areas/swimming-strokes|Swimming Strokes Research]] · Code project: [[projects/swimmer-notes-app|Swimmer's Notes App]]

> [!important] Note on Competitive Targets and Metrics
> All "Competitive target" values in this document are **guidelines**, not absolutes. Actual performance depends on:
> - **Swimmer level** (age-group, collegiate, elite) — tiered targets are provided where applicable
> - **Body type and physiology** — longer limbs, different buoyancy affect metrics
> - **Pool conditions** — water temperature, depth, and current affect times
> - **Measurement method** — hand-timed results have ±0.3s variability; phone video cannot measure sub-centimeter or sub-100ms precision
>
> Metrics requiring high-speed video, motion tracking, or calibrated measurement are noted as such. Focus on **directional improvement** rather than hitting exact numbers.

> [!note] On Difficulty Rankings
> These rankings are general guidelines. Actual difficulty varies by swimmer:
> - **Flutter Kick** (Level 2): Often harder for adult learners than it appears — hip-driven motion is counterintuitive.
> - **Arm Recovery** (Level 6): Arguably easier than Breathing (Level 3) for some swimmers since it's visible and self-correctable. May be reordered based on individual needs.
> - **Pull Phase** (Level 9): Closely tied to The Catch (Level 8). Many coaches treat these as one combined technique. If your catch is good, the pull often follows naturally.

### Image References

**Key Positions to Visualize:**

1. **Streamline Position**
   - Arms pressed together overhead
   - Head between biceps
   - Body completely straight
   - Think: "Arrow position" - minimal width, maximum length

2. **High Elbow Catch (EVF)**
   - Elbow above hand
   - Fingertips pointed down
   - Forearm vertical like a paddle
   - Think: "Anchor your forearm, body moves past it"

3. **Body Rotation (35-50 degrees)**
   - Shoulders and hips rotate together
   - One shoulder higher than other
   - Rotation around spine axis
   - Think: "Log rolling" - rotate as a unit
   - Note: Research by Psycharakis & Sanders (2008) measured elite swimmers at ~30-60 degrees. The 35-50 degree target is the commonly coached optimal range; individual optimal angle depends on event, body type, and flexibility.

4. **Flutter Kick Position**
   - Legs relatively straight (≤30 degrees knee bend at maximum)
   - Small amplitude (12-16 inches / 30-40cm, within body's "shadow")
   - Ankles loose and pointed (elite plantar flexion: 50+ degrees)
   - Think: "Whipping motion from hips"

> [!warning] Quantitative Claims Disclaimer
> Many numerical claims in this document (drag percentages, time savings per race, propulsion ratios) are **estimates** from coaching literature and biomechanics research, not precise scientific measurements. Actual values vary significantly by swimmer. These figures are included to give a sense of relative importance, not as exact physics. Where possible, tiered targets replace single-number "competitive targets" to reflect different skill levels.
>
> **Research-validated claims** are cited with author names and years (e.g., Zamparo et al., 1996; Psycharakis & Sanders, 2008).
> **Coaching heuristics** are noted as such — these are practical guidelines widely used by coaches but not validated by controlled research.
> **Unverifiable claims** (e.g., exact drag reduction percentages, precise propulsion increases from specific technique changes) are flagged with ranges and caveats.
>
> For the most current research, see the sources listed below and recent publications in the *Journal of Sports Sciences*, *Sports Biomechanics*, and *Journal of Biomechanics*.
