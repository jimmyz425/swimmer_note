## Overview
Two kicks per complete arm cycle form the core rhythm of butterfly: a smaller first kick timed with arm entry/catch, and a larger, more powerful second kick timed with the arm push phase. The rhythm should feel like a continuous wave flowing through the body with no pauses between phases.
**Difficulty: Hard**

## Key Points to Remember
- **Two kicks per complete arm cycle**:
  - First kick (smaller): Timing with arm entry/catch
  - Second kick (larger, more powerful): Timing with arm push phase
- Kicks should be continuous and connected
- No pauses in stroke cycle - continuous motion is essential
- The rhythm should feel like a wave flowing through body
- Arm pull and kicks work together for propulsion

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Single kick per cycle (most common beginner error)
- Pausing between stroke phases
- First and second kick equal size
- Timing kicks wrong with arm phases
- Irregular rhythm

## Specific Drills
| Drill | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Kick Count Drill** | Count "one-two" for each kick cycle. Verbalize: "Enter-kick, Push-kick." |
| **3-3-3 Drill** | 3 strokes butterfly, 3 strokes backstroke, 3 strokes breaststroke. Develops rhythm. |
| **Rhythm Swimming** | Swim with emphasis on continuous motion. No pauses between phases. |
| **Slow-Motion Butterfly** | Swim at half speed, exaggerating each phase and kick timing. |
| **Timing Drill with Verbal Cues** | Partner calls out "Enter-Kick, Pull-Kick" while you swim. |

## Competitive Metrics
#### Drill 1: Kick Count Drill (4x100m, count kicks per stroke cycle)
**Self-Check:** Percentage of stroke cycles with exactly 2 kicks (one on entry, one on push)

> [!tip] Tiered Two-Kick Consistency
> | Level | Cycles with Exactly 2 Kicks | Notes |
> |-------|----------------------------|-------|
> | **Beginner** | 60-75% | Single-kick habit still present; practicing rhythm |
> | **Intermediate** | 75-90% | Mostly consistent two-kick pattern |
> | **Advanced** | 90-95% | Reliable two-kick timing at moderate pace |
> | **Elite** | 95-100% | Perfect two-kick timing at all race paces |

- Self-check cue: Kick once when your hands enter water, kick again when your hands push back to your thighs

**Video Check (side-angle footage):**
- Kick timing: First down kick begins as hands enter water (synchronized with entry); second down kick occurs during the arm push phase. Timing precision improves with level -- elite swimmers show near-perfect synchronization.
- Kick power ratio: First kick approximately 60% power, second kick at 100% power (second kick drives body upward for breathing and recovery)
- No delay between kick initiation and arm motion

**Competitive Impact:** Proper two-kick timing per arm cycle is essential for butterfly efficiency. The first kick (smaller, timed with arm entry) stabilizes body position, while the second kick (larger, timed with arm push) adds propulsion and helps lift the body for breathing. Swimming with a single kick per cycle significantly reduces propulsion and disrupts body rhythm. The "20% propulsion increase" figure is a coaching estimate; the actual benefit depends on the swimmer's baseline technique.

#### Drill 2: Metronome Training (4x200m set, use waterproof metronome set to your race tempo)
**Self-Check:** Percentage of stroke cycles that sync exactly with the metronome beep

> [!tip] Tiered Metronome Sync Accuracy
> | Level | Sync Accuracy | Timing Tolerance |
> |-------|--------------|-----------------|
> | **Beginner** | 70-80% | Within 0.15s of target tempo |
> | **Intermediate** | 80-90% | Within 0.10s of target tempo |
> | **Advanced** | 90-95% | Within 0.05s of target tempo |
> | **Elite** | ≥95% | Within 0.03-0.05s of target tempo |

- Self-check cue: Sync the start of your pull exactly with the metronome beep

**Video Check (side-angle footage):**
- Cycle consistency: Stroke cycles maintain consistent duration (Advanced/Elite: ≤5% variation; Beginner: up to 15% acceptable)
- Undulation timing: Body undulation is synced with arm and kick motion
- No timing variation between breathing and non-breathing cycles (elite-level target)

**Competitive Impact:** Consistent tempo throughout a butterfly race is critical. Tempo variation between cycles disrupts rhythm, increases energy cost, and reduces speed. The "0.1s per cycle costs ~1.2s per 100m" claim is a mathematical estimate (0.1s x ~12 cycles per 25m x 4 lengths); actual impact varies with the swimmer's baseline tempo and race distance.
