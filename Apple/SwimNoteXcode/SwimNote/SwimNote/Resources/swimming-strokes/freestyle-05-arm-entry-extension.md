## Overview
Arm entry and extension set up the entire stroke cycle. The hand should enter cleanly in front of the shoulder, fingertips first, and extend fully forward using body rotation to add length to the reach. Proper entry position prevents zigzag swimming patterns and sets up an effective catch.
**Difficulty: Moderate-Hard**

## Key Points to Remember
- **Enter in front of shoulder** - Hand should enter aligned with shoulder, not crossing center line
- **Extend forward fully** - Reach extends after entry, not at entry
- **Fingertips-first, hand relatively flat** - Hand should slice into water cleanly; some swimmers benefit from slight downward finger angle, but avoid steep entry angles
- **Rotate to extend** - Use body rotation to add length to reach
- **Slide in cleanly** - Enter hand smoothly, minimize splash
- **Don't over-reach** - Extension should feel comfortable, not straining

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Crossing center line** - Hand enters across body's midline, causes zigzag path
- **Entering too wide** - Hand enters outside shoulder width
- **Entering thumb-first** - Leads to internal rotation, poor catch setup
- **Slapping entry** - Hand crashes down, creates splash and resistance
- **No extension** - Hand enters and immediately starts pulling
- **Over-reaching** - Reaching past natural extension, causes shoulder strain

## Specific Drills
| Drill | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Entry Placement Drill** | Focus specifically on where hand enters - film yourself or have coach observe. |
| **Clean Entry Drill** | Practice entering with minimal splash, hand slicing water. |
| **Catch-Up Entry** | Catch-up drill, focusing on perfect entry position before starting stroke. |
| **Extension Pause** | After entry, pause 1 second in full extension, feel the reach. |
| **Finger Trail Entry** | Drag fingertips during recovery, then enter clean and extend. |

## Competitive Metrics
#### Drill 1: Entry Placement Drill (use pool lane markers or deck tiles as shoulder-width reference)
**Self-Check:** Count percentage of entries that land approximately at your shoulder line

> [!note] Tiered Targets
> - **Beginner:** ≥70% of entries approximately at shoulder line
> - **Intermediate:** ≥85% of entries at shoulder line
> - **Advanced/Elite:** ≥95% of entries at shoulder line (no crossing center, no wide entry)
>
> Self-check cue: Look briefly forward every 3rd stroke to check entry position. If your hand lands inside your line of sight, you're crossing over.
>
> **Note:** These percentages are coaching heuristics for entry placement consistency. Accurately counting entry percentages during swimming is challenging without video analysis. For practical self-coaching, use the "crossing over" self-check and periodic video review to assess entry position.

**Video Check (top-down footage, if available):**
- Entry quality: Fingertips first, minimal splash, hand slicing into water smoothly
- Extension: Arm extends forward after entry (visible reach before catch begins)
- No cross-over: Hand never enters inside shoulder line (top-down view makes this clear)
- Note: Precise centimeter-level measurement requires calibrated camera setup. Use visual assessment for self-coaching.

**Competitive Impact:** Wide or cross-over entry creates a zig-zag swimming path, increasing total distance traveled. *Estimates suggest 3-5% additional distance per race, though actual impact depends on severity and frequency. Research on crossover entry (Walsh et al., 2006) shows it increases frontal drag and can contribute to shoulder impingement over time. The 3-5% figure is a coaching estimate for moderate crossover; severe crossover may increase distance more.*

#### Drill 2: Extension Pause Drill (4x25m, pause 1s at full extension)
**Self-Check:** Stroke count per 25m when pausing vs normal swimming

> [!note] Targets
> - Competitive target: Similar stroke count to normal swimming (extension doesn't slow rhythm). A small increase (1-2 strokes) is normal for this drill.
> - Self-check cue: Feel the "reach" with your core rotation, not just your arm

**Video Check (side-angle footage):**
- No over-reach: Arm does not extend excessively past shoulder line (comfortable full extension, not strained)
- Clean entry: No splash, hand slices into water
- Competitive target: Smooth transition between extension and catch (no visible hesitation or "dead spot")

**Competitive Impact:** Additional reach per stroke translates to fewer strokes per length. *The relationship between reach and time savings is not linear — hydrodynamic factors mean gains diminish at extreme reach lengths.*
