## Overview
Starts and turns are the most technically complex elements of backstroke. They involve launching backward from the wall, executing underwater dolphin kicks within the 15m limit, counting strokes to know when to rotate for the flip turn, and pushing off in a tight streamline. Mastery of starts and turns provides significant time advantages, particularly in short course pools.

**Difficulty:** Hardest

## Key Points to Remember
- **Feet on wall, hands on gutter** - Launch position for start
- **Launch backward, streamline entry** - Tight streamline for underwater phase
- **Dolphin kicks underwater** - Up to 15m allowed by FINA/World Aquatics rules
- **Transition to stroke smoothly** - No delay after underwater phase
- **Count strokes to wall** - Know your number for turns
- **Rotate to belly at wall approach** - Flip turn or open turn
- **Push off streamline on back** - Dolphin kicks off wall

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Late rotation** - Too close to wall
- **Poor streamline** - Not tight off wall
- **Too few dolphin kicks** - Not utilizing underwater phase
- **Slow transition** - Delayed return to stroke
- **Missing wall** - Stroke count wrong

## Competitive Metrics

#### Drill 1: Underwater Dolphin Kick Set (10x25m, off walls, no surface stroke)
**Self-Check:** Distance traveled off each wall with dolphin kicks before breaking surface

> [!note] Tiered Targets
> - **Beginner:** ≥8m off walls, 4-5 dolphin kicks
> - **Intermediate:** ≥10m off walls, 5-6 dolphin kicks
> - **Advanced:** ≥12m off walls, 6-7 dolphin kicks
> - **Elite:** ≥15m off start walls, ≥12m off turn walls (LCM), 7-8+ dolphin kicks
>
> Self-check cue: Count dolphin kicks per wall. The FINA/World Aquatics 15m rule limits underwater swimming to 15m after starts and turns.
>
> **Note:** These distances are consistent with World Aquatics biomechanical reports from Olympic and World Championship races.

**Video Check (underwater footage):**
- Dolphin kick amplitude: ≤40cm from highest to lowest point. **Note:** Amplitude varies by swimmer size; the key is powerful, compact kicks.
- Kick tempo: Fast, powerful kicks. **Note:** The "≥150 bpm" figure is a coaching estimate. Elite swimmers typically achieve 130-170 bpm for underwater dolphin kicks, with significant individual variation.
- Competitive target: Body remains in streamlined position for entire underwater phase

**Competitive Impact:** Underwater dolphin kicks are faster than surface swimming. **Correction:** The "30% faster" figure is a general coaching estimate; actual speed advantage depends on kick quality. The "contributing ~15% of total race speed" is also an estimate — the actual contribution varies by event distance, number of turns, and individual kick proficiency. World Aquatics race analyses consistently show that swimmers with strong underwater dolphin kicking gain significant time advantages, particularly in short course pools.

#### Drill 2: Turn Timing Drill (10x50m, all turns)
**Self-Check:** Time from hand touch on wall to feet push-off

> [!note] Tiered Targets (Touch-to-Pushoff Wall Contact Time)
> - **Beginner:** ≤1.5s per turn
> - **Intermediate:** ≤1.0s per turn
> - **Advanced:** ≤0.8s per turn
> - **Elite:** ≤0.40s per turn (world-class sprinters often achieve ~0.28-0.35s)
>
> Self-check cue: Focus on quick rotation and explosive push-off
>
> **Correction:** The original stated "elite swimmers hit ≤0.6s." World Aquatics biomechanical reports show elite backstrokers achieve touch-to-pushoff times of 0.25-0.40 seconds, not 0.6s. The 0.6s target is more appropriate for advanced (non-elite) swimmers.

**Video Check (side-angle footage):**
- Flip rotation: Efficient rotation with no wasted movement (one smooth rotation to feet on wall)
- Push-off angle: Shallow, fast push-off (visual estimate — body should leave wall at a near-horizontal angle)
- Competitive target: Quick return to streamline and stroking after push-off

**Competitive Impact:** Faster turns save time per race. *The "0.1s faster per turn = ~0.4s saved per 100m, ~1.6s saved per 400m" claim is a coaching estimate. Actual savings depend on pool length (4 turns per 100m in short course, 2 in long course) and how turn time is measured (wall contact vs. 15m split).*
