# Pool-Test External Focus Cues — 8-Year-Old Boy

> Multiple options per issue. Try them, watch the kid's reaction, keep what works.
> **The only rule that matters:** If his swimming gets better, the cue is good. If it doesn't, try another one.
>
> Generated: 2026-05-12

---

## How to Use This File

1. **Pick ONE issue to work on per session.** Don't try to fix everything.
2. **Try 2-3 different cues for that issue across different lengths.**
3. **Watch his body, not his face.** Did the movement change? That's your answer.
4. **Some cues will land, some won't.** That's normal. Even the same cue works differently on different days.
5. **Write down which ones worked.** Come back to them next time.

## How to Test Cues

### The 3-Length Test

1. **Length 1:** Give cue A. Watch what happens.
2. **Length 2:** Rest. Say nothing.
3. **Length 3:** Give cue B. Watch what happens.
4. **Compare:** Which length showed better movement?

Repeat on another day — consistency across days matters more than one good length.

### Signs a Cue Is Working

- ✅ His body position changes immediately
- ✅ He doesn't ask "what do you mean?"
- ✅ The change lasts for the next length without a reminder
- ✅ He smiles or says "I felt that"

### Signs a Cue Is NOT Working

- ❌ He stops and asks questions
- ❌ His swimming gets worse or more tense
- ❌ Nothing changes after 2-3 tries
- ❌ He looks confused
