# Backstroke Dry Land Training

> Part of [[swimming-strokes/backstroke|Backstroke Swimming Technique Reference]]

These exercises can be done outside the pool to develop strength, flexibility, and muscle memory for backstroke techniques.

---

## Core & Body Position Drills

| Drill | Description | Focus Points |
|-------|-------------|--------------|
| **Back Plank** | Lie on back, prop up on elbows and heels, body straight. Hold 30-45 seconds. 3 sets. | Exact position for backstroke - body flat, hips high. Keep head neutral (mimics head position in water). Core engaged to prevent hips sagging. |
| **Glute Bridge** | Lie on back, knees bent, feet flat. Lift hips by squeezing glutes. Hold 3 seconds. 3 sets of 15. | Hip lift strength - keeps hips high in backstroke. Strong glutes prevent sitting position in water. Feel the hip elevation. |
| **Superman Hold** | Lie face-down, arms and legs extended. Lift arms and legs together. Hold 15-30 seconds. 3 sets. | Back strength for body position on back. Keep body long and engaged. Mimics the extended position during stroke. |

## Rotation Drills

| Drill | Description | Focus Points |
|-------|-------------|--------------|
| **Medicine Ball Side Throws** | Stand sideways to wall/partner, throw med ball across body. 3 sets of 10 each side. | Rotation power for backstroke. Rotate from hips/core, not just arms. Feel the hip-led rotation power. |
| **Russian Twist on Back** | Lie on back, knees bent. Rotate torso touching floor on each side. 3 sets of 20. | Core rotation for body roll. Keep hips stable while rotating torso. Same as keeping legs steady while body rolls. |
| **Standing Rotation Drill (Back)** | Stand tall, rotate shoulders and hips together 30-45 degrees. Arms simulate entry position. 20 reps. | Practice the rotation angle (30-45 degrees, though research shows elite swimmers range 30-60 degrees). Feel the unit rotation - shoulders and hips together. |

---

## Shoulder & Arm Drills

| Drill | Description | Focus Points |
|-------|-------------|--------------|
| **Pinky Entry Drill (Standing)** | Stand, simulate arm recovery: thumb leads up, rotate to pinky-down for entry. 20 reps each arm. | Practice the thumb-to-pinky rotation muscle memory. Pinky enters first every time - this is critical for backstroke. Feel the hand rotation. |
| **Bent-Arm Recovery Drill** | Stand, simulate backstroke arm recovery with bent elbow leading, hand trailing below. Keep arm close to body. 20 reps each arm. | Bent-arm recovery during recovery - elbow leads, hand trails below. Arm close to body line - not wide. Feel the relaxed, efficient motion. |
| **Resistance Band Pull (Back)** | Stand with band behind you, pull forward and down simulating catch and pull. 3 sets of 15 each arm. | Backstroke pull mechanics. Feel a relatively straight pull path (not exaggerated S-curve). Accelerate through the motion. Finish at hip. |
| **Shoulder External Rotation** | Hold resistance band, elbows at sides bent 90 degrees, rotate hands outward. 3 sets of 15. | Shoulder health for backstroke. External rotation strength protects shoulders during pinky-first entry. Prevents impingement. |

## Ankle & Kick Drills

| Drill | Description | Focus Points |
|-------|-------------|--------------|
| **Ankle Point/Flex** | Sit or lie down, point toes hard, then flex back. Hold each 5 seconds. 3 sets of 20. | Ankle flexibility for kick propulsion. Pointed toes during kick = propulsion. Flexible ankles = efficient kick. |
| **Calf Raises (Straight)** | Stand, rise up on toes, hold, lower. 3 sets of 15. | Calf and ankle strength for kick. Strong calves power flutter kick. Builds ankle stability. |
| **Ankle Alphabet** | Sit, trace alphabet with toes. Full range of motion. | Ankle mobility and flexibility. Loose ankles are essential. Each letter = full range of motion. |

---

## Head Position Drills

| Drill | Description | Focus Points |
|-------|-------------|--------------|
| **Wall Head Position Drill** | Lie on floor, head on small cushion. Look straight up at ceiling. Hold 1 minute. | Practice neutral head position. Head resting naturally - not forced up or down. This is the position for backstroke. |
| **Chin Tuck Drill** | Stand or sit, gently tuck chin slightly. Hold 5 seconds, release. 10 reps. | Neutral neck position. Chin slightly tucked, not forced. Prevents looking forward during backstroke. |

---

## Flexibility Drills

| Drill | Description | Focus Points |
|-------|-------------|--------------|
| **Chest Stretch** | Stand in doorway, arms on door frame, lean forward. Hold 30 seconds. | Chest flexibility for arm reach. Open chest allows full arm extension. Tight chest limits entry position. |
| **Hip Flexor Stretch** | Kneeling lunge, push hips forward. Hold 30 seconds each side. | Hip flexibility for rotation. Backstroke needs approximately 30-45 degree rotation each side (research range: 30-60 degrees for elite swimmers per Psycharakis & Sanders, 2008). Flexible hips allow proper rotation. |
| **Shoulder Stretch (Cross-body)** | Pull arm across chest with other hand. Hold 30 seconds each arm. | Shoulder flexibility for recovery. Open shoulders allow proper arm position. Prevents shoulder strain. |
