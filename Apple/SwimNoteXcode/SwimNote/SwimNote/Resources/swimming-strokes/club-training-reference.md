# USA Swimming Club Training Reference

## Quick-Reference Summary Table

| Group | Age Range | Practices/Week | Practice Duration | Weekly Distance | Primary Focus |
|-------|-----------|----------------|-------------------|-----------------|---------------|
| Pre-Competitive | 5-8 | 2-3 | 45-60 min | 3-8 km | Water comfort, basic strokes, fun |
| Bronze | 8-10 | 3-4 | 60-75 min | 8-18 km | Stroke technique, all 4 strokes, first B times |
| Silver | 10-12 | 4-5 | 75-90 min | 15-28 km | Refinement, aerobic base, A times |
| Gold | 12-14 | 5-6 | 90-105 min | 25-40 km | Threshold intro, race strategy, AA times |
| Senior | 14-18 | 6-8 | 90-120 min | 40-60 km | Race pace, lactate work, AAA-AAAA times |
| National | 14+ | 8-12+ | 120-180 min | 50-80+ km | Peak performance, race simulation |

> Distances in km. 1 km = 1,000 meters = ~1,094 yards. Weekly distances vary by season.

## Group Details

### Pre-Competitive / Developmental

**Alt names:** Learn-to-Swim, Minnows, Novice, Swim 101, Foundations

**Training:**
- Practices/week: 2-3 | Duration: 45-60 min | Weekly distance: 3-8 km
- Lane organization: 4-8 swimmers per lane | Coach ratio: 1:6 to 1:8

**Training Focus:**
- 60-70% — Stroke technique drills (all four strokes)
- 15-20% — Starts and turns
- 10-15% — Games, relays, fun
- 0-5% — Timed swimming

**Zone Distribution:**

| Zone 0 | Zone 1 | Zone 2 | Zone 3 | Zone 4+ |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|---------|
| 10-15% | 55-65% | 15-20% | 5-10% | 0-5% |

**Qualification Criteria:**
- Swim 25 yards freestyle with side breathing (no stopping)
- Swim 25 yards backstroke with alternating arm motion
- Tread water 1-2 minutes
- Submerge face and blow bubbles

**Promotion to Bronze:**
- All four strokes recognizable for 25 yards each
- Complete 30-min practice without stopping
- Demonstrates lane etiquette

---

### Bronze / Junior Age Group

**Alt names:** Age Group 1, Junior, Bronze Squad, Novice Competitive, Blue Group

**Training:**
- Practices/week: 3-4 | Duration: 60-75 min | Weekly distance: 8-18 km
- Lane organization: 4-6 swimmers per lane | Coach ratio: 1:8 to 1:10

**Training Focus:**
- 40-50% — Stroke technique refinement
- 20-25% — Aerobic base building
- 15-20% — Starts, turns, underwater skills
- 10-15% — Interval training introduction
- 0-5% — Sprint/race-pace work

**Zone Distribution:**

| Zone 0 | Zone 1 | Zone 2 | Zone 3 | Zone 4 | Zone 5-6 |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|------|----------|
| 10% | 50-55% | 20-25% | 10-15% | 0-5% | 0% |

**Qualification Criteria:**
- Proficiency in all four competitive strokes (25 yards each, legal technique)
- Swim 100-200 yards continuously
- Basic flip turn

**Promotion to Silver:**
- B times in at least 1-2 events
- Consistent stroke mechanics across all 4 strokes at practice pace
- Complete full 75-min practice with focus

---

### Silver / Age Group

**Alt names:** Age Group 2, Silver Squad, Intermediate, White Group

**Training:**
- Practices/week: 4-5 | Duration: 75-90 min | Weekly distance: 15-28 km
- Per-practice distance: 3-5.5 km (3,000-5,500 yards)
- Lane organization: 4-6 swimmers per lane, grouped by pace

**Training Focus:**
- 30-35% — Stroke technique refinement
- 30-35% — Aerobic base and endurance
- 15-20% — Threshold introduction (CSS pace work, 100s and 200s)
- 10-15% — Starts, turns, underwater skills, race strategy
- 5-10% — Sprint work and race-pace activities

**Zone Distribution:**

| Zone 0 | Zone 1 | Zone 2 | Zone 3 | Zone 4 | Zone 5 | Zone 6 |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|------|------|--------|
| 10% | 40-45% | 25-30% | 10-15% | 5-10% | 0-5% | 0% |

**Qualification Criteria:**
- B times in at least 2 events
- All four strokes legal for 50+ yards each
- Complete 2,000-3,000 yards in single practice

**Promotion to Gold:**
- A times in at least 2 events (working toward AA)
- Handle 5 practices per week consistently
- Technical consistency at faster paces

---

### Gold / Senior Age Group

**Alt names:** Age Group 3, Gold Squad, Pre-Senior, Red Group

**Training:**
- Practices/week: 5-6 | Duration: 90-105 min | Weekly distance: 25-40 km
- Per-practice distance: 4.5-7 km (5,000-7,500 yards)
- Double sessions: 0-2 days per week

**Training Focus:**
- 25-30% — Stroke-specific technique refinement
- 30-35% — Aerobic base and endurance
- 15-20% — Threshold training (CSS work, pace clock intervals)
- 10-15% — Race-pace work and sprint development
- 10% — Starts, turns, underwaters, race strategy
- 5-10% — Dryland training

**Zone Distribution:**

| Zone 0 | Zone 1 | Zone 2 | Zone 3 | Zone 4 | Zone 5 | Zone 6 |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|------|------|--------|
| 10% | 35-40% | 25-30% | 15-20% | 5-10% | 0-5% | 0-3% |

**Qualification Criteria:**
- A times in at least 2-3 events
- Complete 3,000-4,000 yards in single practice with good technique

**Promotion to Senior:**
- AA times in at least 2 events (working toward AAA)
- Handle 6+ practices per week
- Readiness for double sessions

---

### Senior / Championship Group

**Alt names:** Senior Squad, Championship, Varsity, Black Group

**Training:**
- Practices/week: 6-8 | Duration: 90-120 min | Weekly distance: 40-60 km
- Per-practice distance: 5.5-8.5 km (6,000-9,000 yards)
- Double sessions: 2-4 days per week

**Training Focus:**
- 20-25% — Stroke-specific technique (fine-tuning at race pace)
- 25-30% — Aerobic endurance
- 20-25% — Threshold and race-pace work (CSS, T-pace)
- 10-15% — VO2max and lactate tolerance
- 10% — Sprint work and speed development
- 10% — Starts, turns, underwaters, race strategy, mental skills

**Zone Distribution:**

| Zone 0 | Zone 1 | Zone 2 | Zone 3 | Zone 4 | Zone 5 | Zone 6 |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|------|------|--------|
| 10% | 25-30% | 25-30% | 15-20% | 10-15% | 5-10% | 3-5% |

**Qualification Criteria:**
- AA or AAA times in at least 2 events
- Complete 5,000-6,000 yards in single practice with maintained technique
- Commitment to morning and afternoon practices

**Promotion to National:**
- AAA or AAAA times in at least 2 events
- Qualifies for Junior Nationals or Senior Nationals

---

### National / Elite Group

**Alt names:** Elite Squad, Performance Group, National Team, High Performance

**Training:**
- Practices/week: 8-12+ | Duration: 120-180 min | Weekly distance: 50-80+ km
- Per-practice distance: 5-10 km (5,000-10,000+ yards)
- Double sessions: 5-6 days per week

**Training Focus:**
- 15-20% — Technique fine-tuning (video analysis, biomechanical optimization)
- 20-25% — Aerobic maintenance
- 20-25% — Threshold and race-pace work (event-specific)
- 15-20% — VO2max and lactate tolerance
- 10-15% — Sprint and speed development
- 10% — Starts, turns, underwaters, race strategy, mental skills

**Zone Distribution:**

| Zone 0 | Zone 1 | Zone 2 | Zone 3 | Zone 4 | Zone 5 | Zone 6 |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|------|------|--------|
| 10% | 15-20% | 20-25% | 15-20% | 15-20% | 10-15% | 5-10% |

**Qualification Criteria:**
- AAAA times or National qualifying cuts in at least 2 events
- Invited by head coach
- Exceptional commitment to training, academics, team culture

## Training Zone Definitions

| Zone | Name | Description |
|------|------|-------------|
| Zone 0 | Recovery | Active rest between sets |
| Zone 1 | Aerobic Base | Easy, conversational pace |
| Zone 2 | Aerobic Endurance | Moderate, steady swimming |
| Zone 3 | Tempo | Comfortably hard |
| Zone 4 | Lactate Threshold | Hard, structured threshold sets |
| Zone 5 | VO2max | Very hard, race-pace and over-pace |
| Zone 6 | Sprint | Maximum effort, speed work |

## Club Groups to 4-Level Categorization

| Club Group | 4-Level Mapping | USA Swimming Standard | Competition Level |
|------------|-----------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Pre-Competitive | Pre-Level 1 | None required | Intra-squad |
| Bronze | Level 1 (Beginner) | B times | Local / dual meets |
| Silver | Level 1-2 (transition) | B-A times | Sectional / LSC championship |
| Gold | Level 2 (Intermediate) | A-AA times | Sectional / Zone / Junior qualifier |
| Senior | Level 2-3 (Intermediate-Advanced) | AA-AAA times | Zone / Junior/Senior Nationals |
| National | Level 3-4 (Advanced-Elite) | AAAA / National cuts | National / International |

> The 4-level system is stroke-specific; a swimmer can be Level 3 in freestyle and Level 1 in butterfly. Club groups are whole-swimmer categorizations.

## LTAD Stages and Club Group Mapping

| LTAD Stage | Age (Males) | Age (Females) | Club Group(s) | Key Development Focus |
|------------|-------------|---------------|---------------|----------------------|
| Active Start | 0-6 | 0-6 | Pre-Comp (youngest) | Water comfort, play-based learning |
| FUNdamentals | 6-8 | 6-7 | Pre-Competitive | Basic strokes, water safety, fun |
| Learn to Train | 8-11 | 8-10 | Bronze, Silver (younger) | All four strokes, basic competition |
| Train to Train | 11-14 | 10-13 | Silver (older), Gold | Aerobic base building, technical refinement |
| Train to Compete | 14-18 | 13-17 | Senior, National (younger) | Event specialization, high-intensity training |
| Train to Win | 18+ | 17+ | National (older) | Peak performance, individualized training |

### Key LTAD Principles

1. Chronological age is not developmental age. Group by ability and maturity.
2. Multi-sport participation encouraged through Learn to Train phase.
3. Golden age of skill learning: ages 8-12 (girls), 9-13 (boys).
4. Aerobic base should be built BEFORE high-intensity training.
5. Competition format should match developmental stage.

## Coaching Principles (Conclusions Only)

### When to Introduce High-Intensity Training

- **Before age 10-11:** No formal high-intensity training. All work in Zones 0-3, majority in Zones 1-2.
- **Ages 11-14 (Train to Train):** Introduction to threshold work (Zone 3-4) in limited amounts (10-20%). Focus remains on aerobic development. VO2max work minimal (0-5%).
- **Ages 14+ (Train to Compete):** Systematic introduction of all training zones. Threshold work 10-20%. VO2max work 5-15%. Sprint work 3-10%.

### Volume Progression for Young Swimmers

| Age Range | km/Week | Notes |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| 6-8 | 3-8 | Skill acquisition, not volume |
| 8-10 | 8-18 | Gradual increase as endurance develops |
| 10-12 | 15-28 | Golden window for aerobic base building |
| 12-14 | 25-40 | Significant volume increase, primarily Zone 1-2 |
| 14-16 | 40-60 | Introduction of double sessions and higher intensity |
| 16+ | 50-80+ | Full senior/elite training load |

> Rate of volume increase should never exceed 10-15% per week.

### Elite Developmental Program Characteristics

1. **Patient progression:** Swimmers spend 2-3 years in each age group.
2. **Technique before volume:** Stroke mechanics prioritized in pre-competitive and age-group years.
3. **Volume before intensity:** Train to Train focuses on aerobic engine through Zone 1-2. Intensity introduced gradually after aerobic base established.
4. **Clear promotion criteria:** Time standards, attendance, skill assessment, coach evaluation.
5. **Multi-sport encouragement:** Especially through age 12-14.
6. **Coaching education:** ASCA certification, continuing education, mentorship.
7. **Parent education:** LTAD model, dangers of early specialization, importance of patience.

## Club Attrition Funnel

| Group | Approx. % of Club Roster | Notes |
|-------|-------------------------|-------|
| Pre-Competitive | 20-30% | Largest group; many never transition to competitive |
| Bronze | 20-25% | First year; many drop out after 1-2 seasons |
| Silver | 15-20% | Committed; still significant attrition |
| Gold | 10-15% | The "committed core" |
| Senior | 5-10% | Serious competitors; many go to collegiate swimming |
| National | 1-3% | Elite; typically 2-8 swimmers per club |

> For every 100 swimmers who join Pre-Competitive, approximately 1-3 reach National/Elite level.
