# Freestyle Dry Land Training

> Part of [[swimming-strokes/freestyle|Freestyle Swimming Techniques]]

These exercises can be done outside the pool to develop strength, flexibility, and muscle memory for freestyle techniques.

---

## Core & Body Position Drills

| Drill | Description | Focus Points |
|-------|-------------|--------------|
| **Plank Hold** | Hold plank position on forearms, maintain straight body line. 3 sets of 30-60 seconds. | Keep body completely flat - mimics streamlined position. Engage core to prevent hips dropping. Head neutral, looking down. |
| **Side Plank** | Lie on side, prop up on forearm, hold position. 30 seconds each side. | Develops core stability for rotation. Feel the side-body engagement that powers rotation. Keep hips high - don't let them sag. |
| **Dead Bug** | Lie on back, arms up, legs up at 90 degrees. Lower opposite arm/leg alternately. | Core control for stable body position. Keep lower back pressed to floor - mimics keeping hips high in water. Controlled movement. |

---

## Rotation Drills

| Drill | Description | Focus Points |
|-------|-------------|--------------|
| **Medicine Ball Rotational Throws** | Stand with feet planted, rotate torso to throw med ball against wall or to partner. | Rotate from HIPS and CORE, not just shoulders. Feel the power transfer from hips through torso to arms - same as freestyle rotation power. |
| **Russian Twist** | Sit on floor, lean back slightly, rotate torso touching floor on each side with hands or med ball. | Core rotation strength. Keep hips stable while rotating above - same as keeping legs steady while body rotates. Continuous motion. |
| **Standing Rotation Drill** | Stand tall, rotate shoulders and hips together 35-50 degrees each side, no arm swing. 20 reps. | Feel the hip-led rotation. Shoulders and hips move as a unit - exactly what you need in freestyle. Practice within your natural rotation range. |

## Shoulder & Arm Drills

| Drill | Description | Focus Points |
|-------|-------------|--------------|
| **High Elbow Catch Drill (Standing)** | Stand at wall, simulate catch motion: arm forward, drop fingertips, lift elbow. 20 reps each arm. | Practice the high elbow position muscle memory. Feel the fingertips dropping while elbow stays high - this is EVF. Do it slowly to feel the position. |
| **Resistance Band Pull** | Attach band to fixed point, stand facing it. Pull band back with high elbow, accelerate through. 3 sets of 15. | Simulates pull phase mechanics. High elbow throughout, accelerate at end. Feel the back muscles (lats) doing the work, not just arms. |
| **Shoulder Blade Squeeze** | Stand with arms at sides, squeeze shoulder blades together, hold 5 seconds. 3 sets of 10. | Develops scapular stability for proper arm mechanics. Keeps shoulders stable during stroke. Prevents rounded shoulders. |
| **Arm Circle Stretch** | Large arm circles forward and backward, controlled. 10 each direction. | Shoulder mobility for recovery and entry. Keep motion smooth and controlled. Prevents impingement, develops range of motion. |

## Ankle & Kick Drills

| Drill | Description | Focus Points |
|-------|-------------|--------------|
| **Ankle Flexibility Stretch** | Sit on floor, legs extended. Point toes hard, then flex feet back. Hold each 10 seconds. 3 sets. | Develops flexible ankles for propulsion. Pointing = plantar flexion (foot pointed during kick). Flexing = dorsiflexion (builds range). |
| **Calf Raises** | Stand on edge of step, rise up on toes, lower below step level. 3 sets of 15. | Ankle strength and flexibility. Strong calves power the kick. Range of motion develops ankle flexibility. |
| **Ankle Circles** | Sit or lie down, rotate ankles in circles. 20 circles each direction each foot. | Ankle mobility for efficient kick. Loose, flexible ankles create propulsion. Tight ankles create drag. |

---

## Flexibility Drills

| Drill | Description | Focus Points |
|-------|-------------|--------------|
| **Hip Flexor Stretch** | Kneeling lunge position, push hips forward. Hold 30 seconds each side. | Hip flexibility for proper rotation range. Tight hip flexors limit rotation. Freestyle needs approximately 35-50 degrees of rotation each side (research range: 30-60 degrees for elite swimmers). |
| **Lat Stretch** | Stand at wall, place arm up against wall, lean away. Hold 30 seconds each side. | Lat flexibility for arm reach and pull. Lats are primary pulling muscles. Flexible lats allow full arm extension. |
| **Streamline Stretch** | Stand tall, arms extended overhead pressed together. Hold 20 seconds. | Practice streamline position muscle memory. Arms tight together, body tall. Feel the position you'll use off every wall. |
